Chapter 7: Consumers, Producers, and Market Efficiency

Willingness to pay = the most a buyer would pay for one unit. Cost = the least a seller would accept for one unit.

1) Equilibrium and Surplus

At equilibrium, total surplus (consumer + producer) is maximized when there is no market failure.

2) Quantity Too Low

If quantity is below efficient level, some units have buyer value above seller cost, but they are not traded.

3) Quantity Too High

If quantity is above efficient level, extra units have seller cost above buyer value.

Fast Checks

  • Producer surplus is usually positive at equilibrium; it is not zero for all sellers.
  • The highest-cost seller at equilibrium is often near zero producer surplus on the last unit.
  • Consumer surplus area is under demand and above price, from quantity 0 to quantity sold.
  • Efficiency means max total surplus; equality means fairness of distribution. They are different goals.
