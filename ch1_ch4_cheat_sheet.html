Principles of Microeconomics: Chapters 1-4

Exam Cheat Sheet

Backbone in 20 seconds: Opportunity Cost (best forgone) -> Comparative Advantage (lower OC) -> Supply/Demand (price coordinates choices) -> Shift vs Movement (most tested distinction).

Chapter 1: Ten Principles

  1. Trade-offs are unavoidable.
  2. Opportunity cost = best forgone alternative.
  3. Marginal thinking: compare MB vs MC for the next unit.
  4. Incentives change behavior.
  5. Trade can benefit all sides.
  6. Markets usually organize activity well.
  7. Government can improve outcomes with market failure/equity concerns.
  8. Productivity drives living standards; money growth drives inflation (long run).

Trap: cost is not all alternatives, only the best forgone one.

Chapter 2: Thinking Like an Economist

  1. Models simplify to isolate cause/effect.
  2. Circular flow: households sell factors, firms sell goods/services.
  3. PPF: on-curve efficient, inside inefficient, outside unattainable.
  4. Bowed-out PPF means increasing opportunity cost.
  5. Micro vs Macro: market-level vs economy-wide.
  6. Positive = what is; Normative = what ought to be.
  7. Economists disagree due to facts/assumptions or values.

Chapter 3: Interdependence & Trade

  1. Absolute advantage = higher output.
  2. Comparative advantage = lower opportunity cost.
  3. Specialize by comparative advantage, then trade.
  4. Mutual gains require trade price between each side's OC.
  5. Imports = buy from abroad; Exports = sell abroad.
OC of 1 unit of A = (max B output) / (max A output)

Trap: do not assign specialization from absolute advantage.

Chapter 4: Supply, Demand, Equilibrium

  1. Own price changes -> movement along curve.
  2. Non-price changes -> shift curve.
  3. Equilibrium: Qd = Qs.
  4. Price below equilibrium -> shortage (Qd > Qs).
  5. Price above equilibrium -> surplus (Qs > Qd).
  6. Demand shifts right (supply fixed) -> P up, Q up.
